Headline Finding

The Personal Empowerment course produces significant, durable gains in emotional intelligence — and the effect has held steady across five years.

Across 468 matched pre/post pairs from 2021–2025, PE students gained +3.9 points on Total EQ (p < .001, d = 0.49), while 394 matched control students showed essentially zero change (+0.3, not significant). Every single competency and every life outcome favored the PE group, all at p < .01 or better. This isn’t a one-off result — PE students outperformed controls in every year of the study.

Self-Awareness Leads the Way

The biggest competency gains are in the “Know Yourself” domain

Emotional Literacy (+6.4), Recognize Patterns (+5.7), and Consequential Thinking (+5.2) show the largest pre-to-post gains. These self-awareness competencies are foundational to the Six Seconds model, and the course clearly excels at building them.

Protective Effect

Control students declined on Wellbeing and Quality of Life — PE students didn’t

The control group showed significant declines in Wellbeing (−1.7, p < .001) and Quality of Life (−1.5, p = .003) over the semester — a pattern consistent with typical college stress. PE students were buffered from this decline, holding steady or improving. The course appears to build resilience, not just skills.

Strategic Opportunity

The competencies with the biggest gains aren’t the strongest predictors of life outcomes

Exercise Optimism, Intrinsic Motivation, and Noble Goals are the strongest predictors of Effectiveness, Relationship, Quality of Life, and Wellbeing in regression analysis — but the course produces its largest gains in Emotional Literacy and Recognize Patterns. Strengthening the Choose/Give Yourself curriculum components could amplify impact on student wellbeing.

The Empathy Puzzle

Empathy is the one competency that resists growth

PE students gained just +0.7 on Empathy (not significant), while the control group actually declined −1.9 (p < .001). Empathy is also the most volatile competency year to year, and the weakest predictor of most outcomes. Its interpersonal nature may make it harder to develop through coursework alone.

Standout Year

2024 was the strongest year across nearly every measure

Total EQ gained +5.1 (d = 0.60) in 2024, with Wellbeing jumping +3.8 (vs. near-zero in prior years). Something about the 2024 cohort or curriculum delivery worked especially well — worth investigating what was different. Meanwhile, outcome improvements have been trending upward across all five years.

Consistency

Baselines are stable, and the effect doesn’t vary by semester

Incoming students’ pre-test scores haven’t meaningfully shifted from 2021–2025, so the course is working with similar raw material each year. Fall and spring semesters produce nearly identical gains overall, with one exception: Exercise Optimism gains are significantly larger in spring (+4.3 vs. +2.1, p = .03).

Questions & Next Steps

What was different about 2024?
The gains were notably stronger that year. Was there a curriculum change, a different instructor emphasis, or a cohort composition difference that could be replicated?
Can Empathy be moved?
The one competency that resists growth. Would adding peer-based or experiential activities (like the POP-UP Festival) specifically targeting empathy make a difference?
Gender & subgroup effects?
With 615 women and 563 men in the PE data, is there a differential effect? Do certain demographic groups benefit more from the course?
Brain Profiles & Agility?
The dataset includes Brain Profile types and 16 brain talent scores. Do certain profiles gain more from the course? Does Brain Agility change?
Faculty/staff cohort?
49 faculty/staff completed the PE training across three cohorts. Small sample, but do they show similar patterns to students, or is the adult learner experience different?
Connection to the POP-UP Festival?
The POP-UP study showed that leading an EQ event amplified Effectiveness. Could combining PE coursework with the POP-UP leadership experience produce compounding effects?

Key Insight
Across all four outcomes, Exercise Optimism, Intrinsic Motivation, and Noble Goals are consistently the strongest predictors — yet the course produces its largest gains in Emotional Literacy and Recognize Patterns (the self-awareness competencies). This suggests an opportunity to strengthen the Choose/Give Yourself curriculum components to maximize downstream impact on student life outcomes.
Methodology: Pre/post pairs matched by student name within academic year (avg ~90 days between assessments). Statistical tests: independent samples t-test for PE vs Control change scores; paired t-test for within-group pre→post. Regression: OLS with standardized predictors on PE pre-test data (n=655). Effect sizes reported as Cohen’s d. All competency and outcome differences between PE and Control significant at p < .01 or better. 2025 data is partial (spring post-tests and fall cohorts still incoming).
